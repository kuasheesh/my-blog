## 2. üß© Basic Features of the Indian Economy

### **1. Low Per Capita Income (The Paradox of Size)**

* **The Feature:** While India is the **5th largest economy** in the world (Nominal GDP ~$4.19 Trillion), it ranks **~136th** in Per Capita Income.
* **Data Point:** Per Capita Income is approx **$2,820 (Nominal)** vs. Global Average of ~$13,000.
* **Meaning:** "Rich India, Poor Indians." The aggregate wealth is high, but it is divided among 1.4 billion people.

### **2. Demographic Dividend (The Youth Power)**

* **The Feature:** India has the world's largest young population.
* **Median Age:** ~28 years (compared to 38 in USA, 48 in Japan).
* **Opportunity:** Massive labor force availability for the next 2-3 decades.
* **Risk:** If jobs aren't created, this dividend becomes a "Demographic Disaster."

### **3. K-Shaped Recovery & Inequality**

* **The Feature:** Post-pandemic growth has been uneven.
* **The "K":** The upper arm (Formal sector, IT, Stock Market) is growing fast, while the lower arm (Informal sector, MSMEs, rural wages) is recovering slowly.

### **4. "Leapfrog" Development**

* Unlike the West, which moved from **Agri \rightarrow Industry \rightarrow Services**, India moved from **Agri \rightarrow Services**, largely skipping the heavy industrialization phase.

## 3. üìà Trends in National Income

### **A. Growth Trajectory**

India is currently the **fastest-growing major economy** in the world.

* **Growth Rate:** Projected at **6.5% - 7%** for FY25-26.
* **Trend:** Resilience against global recessionary trends.

### **B. Sectoral Shifts (Structural Change)**

1. **Declining Share of Agriculture:** In 1950, Agri was ~55% of GDP. Today it is <18%. *Note: This is a sign of a developing economy.*
2. **Servicification of Economy:** Services now contribute nearly 55%. This is driven by high-skill sectors like IT and low-skill sectors like construction/retail.
3. **Jobless Growth Concern:** While GDP grows at 7%, employment grows much slower (<1%). We are producing value without producing enough commensurate jobs.

### **C. Per Capita Income Trends**

* **Rising Slowly:** It has doubled in the last decade but remains in the "Lower Middle Income" category.
* **Purchasing Power Parity (PPP):** In PPP terms, India looks much better (3rd largest economy), meaning money buys more locally than the exchange rate suggests.

## 4. üìù Critical Observations (For Exam/Analysis)

> **The "Disguised Unemployment" Problem:**
> * **Scenario:** A farm needs 2 people, but 5 people work on it.
> * **Result:** Marginal productivity of the extra 3 workers is **ZERO**. If you remove them, farm output won't drop. This is rampant in Indian agriculture.
> 
> 

> **The "Missing Middle" in Industry:**
> * India has huge giants (Tata, Reliance) and millions of tiny micro-enterprises. It lacks the "Mid-sized" companies (Mittelstand) that drive employment in countries like Germany.
> 
>
