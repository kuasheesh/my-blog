# üåè India & Its Neighborhood: The Ring of Fire & Friendship

> **"You can change your friends, but not your neighbors."**
> ‚Äî *Atal Bihari Vajpayee (Former PM of India)*

### üöÄ The Core Philosophy: "Neighborhood First"

India‚Äôs foreign policy revolves around the belief that a peaceful periphery is essential for domestic growth.

* **The Gujral Doctrine (1996):** The gold standard of Indian diplomacy. It advocates **non-reciprocity**‚ÄîIndia gives more to its smaller neighbors without expecting immediate returns.
* **SAGAR (2015):** *Security and Growth for All in the Region*. Focuses on the maritime neighborhood (Indian Ocean).
* **The Punchline:** *India acts as the "Net Security Provider" and the "First Responder" in crises.*

---

### 1. The Troubled Western Front: Pakistan üáµüá∞

*The Sibling Rivalry*

* **Current Status:** "Cold Peace." Diplomatic ties are downgraded; trade is suspended.
* **The 3 Ts:** **T**errorism, **T**rade (lack thereof), and **T**erritory (Kashmir).
* **Key Stance:** India maintains that "Terror and talks cannot go together."
* **Bone of Contention:** Cross-border terrorism, Indus Water Treaty disagreements.

---

### 2. The Dragon in the Room: China üá®üá≥

*The Strategic Competitor*

* **The Reality:** An "armed coexistence." The relationship is defined by the 3 Cs: **C**ooperation (Trade), **C**ompetition (Influence), and **C**onflict (Border).
* **Flashpoints:**
* **The LAC (Line of Actual Control):** Galwan Valley clash (2020) froze relations.
* **String of Pearls:** China‚Äôs encirclement of India through ports in Sri Lanka, Pakistan, and Myanmar.


* **Trade Paradox:** Despite border tensions, China remains one of India's largest trading partners.

---

### 3. The Himalayan Buffers: Nepal & Bhutan

#### **Nepal üá≥üáµ** (*Roti-Beti ka Rishta*)

* **The Bond:** Open borders, strong cultural ties (Bread and Daughter relationship).
* **The Irritant:** The **Kalapani-Limpiyadhura** map dispute and China‚Äôs increasing footprint in Kathmandu.
* **Punchline:** *India is the brother, but Nepal hates the "Big Brother" attitude.*

#### **Bhutan üáßüáπ** (*The All-Weather Friend*)

* **The Bond:** Hydropower cooperation (India buys power, Bhutan gets revenue).
* **Strategic Value:** The **Doklam Plateau** (tri-junction) is critical for India's Siliguri Corridor (Chicken's Neck) security.
* **Status:** A model relationship of mutual trust.

---

### 4. The Eastern Gateway: Bangladesh & Myanmar

#### **Bangladesh üáßüá©** (*The Success Story*)

* **Golden Era:** Known as the *Shonali Adhyay*.
* **Pillars:** Connectivity (Rail/Bus), Energy pipeline, and settling the Land Boundary Agreement (2015).
* **Friction:** The Teesta River water sharing and the issue of illegal migration.
* **Quote:** *"A model of how neighbors should resolve disputes."*

#### **Myanmar üá≤üá≤** (*The Bridge to ASEAN*)

* **Projects:** Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project.
* **Challenge:** The Military Junta rule and the Rohingya refugee crisis affecting India's Northeast.

* **Recent History:** India provided $4 Billion in aid during the 2022 Sri Lankan economic collapse.
* **Fishermen Issue:** Regular arrests of Indian fishermen in the Palk Strait remain a humanitarian issue.
* **Punchline:** *When China asked for collateral, India sent cash.*

#### **Maldives üá≤üáª** (*The Strategic Tollgate*)

* **Geopolitics:** Located near critical shipping lanes.
* **Politics:** Fluctuates between "India First" and "India Out" campaigns depending on the ruling party.
* **Current Vibe:** Tense, due to the new government's pro-China tilt.

### ‚ö†Ô∏è The Challenges (SWOT Analysis)

* **The China Factor:** Beijing's deep pockets (Belt and Road Initiative) are hard to match financially.
* **Delivery Deficit:** India often announces projects but delays execution (unlike China's rapid infrastructure building).
* **Domestic Politics:** Internal issues (like CAA/NRC) sometimes spill over, upsetting neighbors like Bangladesh and Afghanistan.

### üí° The Way Forward: "Connect, Commerce, Culture"

1. **Soft Power:** Leverage Yoga, Bollywood, and Buddhism.
2. **Infrastructure:** Finish the projects (roads, rails, pipelines) fast.
3. **Non-Reciprocity:** Continue helping without asking for immediate returns (Vaccine Maitri was a great example).

> **Final Thought:**
> India cannot become a global superpower if it is bogged down in a hostile neighborhood. The path to the global high table goes through the streets of Dhaka, Colombo, and Kathmandu.

### üî• The "Bone of Contention": Major Irritants

#### 1. Kashmir (The K-Word)

* **Pakistan's View:** It is the "unfinished business of Partition."
* **India's View:** An integral part of India; the only issue is POJK (Pakistan Occupied J&K).
* **Current Status:** Post-August 2019 (Abrogation of Art 370), Pakistan suspended trade and downgraded ties.

* **The Doctrine:** Pakistan uses terror as a state policy ("Bleed India with a thousand cuts").

* **Punchline:** *Terror and Talks cannot go together.*

* **The Treaty:** Brokered by the World Bank. Often called the world's most successful water treaty because it survived three wars.
* **The Split:** India gets Eastern Rivers (Ravi, Beas, Sutlej); Pakistan gets Western Rivers (Indus, Jhelum, Chenab).
* **Current Friction:** India building dams (Kishenganga, Ratle) which Pakistan opposes. India threatens to "maximize" its usage.

# üè≠ The "Carbon Wall" of Europe: CBAM & Indian Steel

> **"It is trade protectionism disguised as climate concern. They polluted for 200 years, and now they want to tax us for developing."**
> ‚Äî *Piyush Goyal (Commerce Minister of India)*

### üåç What is CBAM? (The "Green Tariff")

**Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)** is essentially an import duty imposed by the European Union on carbon-intensive products.

* **The Logic:** If a factory in India emits more CO‚ÇÇ than a factory in Europe to make the same steel rod, the Indian exporter must pay a "Carbon Tax" to enter the EU market.
* **The Goal:** Prevent "Carbon Leakage" (i.e., stopping European companies from moving factories to countries with weaker environmental laws).
* **The Timeline:**
* **Transitional Phase:** Oct 2023 ‚Äì Dec 2025 (Reporting only, no money involved).
* **Definitive Phase:** **Starts Jan 1, 2026** (The tax must be paid).



---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Why is Indian Steel in the Crosshairs?

Steel is the backbone of Indian exports to the EU, but it is also the "dirtiest" in terms of production methods compared to Europe.

#### **The "Coal" Trap**

* **Europe's Method:** Uses **Electric Arc Furnaces (EAF)** powered by green energy or recycled scrap.
* **India's Method:** Uses **Blast Furnaces (BF-BOF)** powered by **Coal**.
* **The Math:**
* Global Average Emission: ~1.9 tonnes of CO‚ÇÇ per tonne of steel.
* **India's Emission:** ~2.5 tonnes of CO‚ÇÇ per tonne of steel.
* **EU's Threshold:** Much lower.


* **The Punchline:** *India makes steel with coal; Europe makes steel with scraps. Now Europe wants to tax the coal.*

---

### üí∞ The Financial Hit (The Numbers)

As we approach the **Jan 2026** deadline, the anxiety in Mumbai and Odisha is real.

| Metric | The Impact |
| --- | --- |
| **Export Exposure** | ~25-30% of India's steel exports go to the EU. |
| **Estimated Tax** | **20% to 35%** additional cost on Indian Steel. |
| **Cost Per Tonne** | Estimated **$100 - $190** levy per tonne. |
| **Total Liability** | Indian exporters may face a bill of **‚Ç¨300 - ‚Ç¨500 Million** annually. |

**The Risk:** Indian steel becomes too expensive for European buyers, who might switch to "cleaner" steel from countries like Turkey or Brazil.

---

### üáÆüá≥ India‚Äôs Counter-Strike (2025 Developments)

India is not taking this lying down. The response is a mix of diplomacy, technology, and market shifting.

#### **1. The "Green Steel" Taxonomy (Dec 2025)**

To prove its intent, India introduced its own rating system.

* **The System:** A **5-Star Rating** for steel based on emissions.
* **The Goal:** Create a domestic label that the EU might eventually recognize to reduce the tax burden.
* *5-Star:* < 1.6 tonnes CO‚ÇÇ/tonne (Elite/Green).
* *3-Star:* 2.0 - 2.2 tonnes CO‚ÇÇ/tonne (Average).



#### **2. The "Carbon Credit" Shield**

* India launched the **Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS)**.
* **The Logic:** If Indian companies pay a carbon price *in India*, the EU allows a deduction on the CBAM tax. India wants the money to stay in New Delhi, not go to Brussels.

#### **3. Market Diversification (Plan B)**

* If Europe closes the door, India opens a window elsewhere.
* **New Markets:** Aggressive push into the **Middle East (UAE/Saudi)** and **Africa** where infrastructure growth is booming and "Green" certificates aren't mandatory yet.

---

### ‚öñÔ∏è The "Bone of Contention": CBDR

* **The Principle:** **Common But Differentiated Responsibilities (CBDR)**.
* **India's Argument:** "You (The West) built your skyscrapers using dirty steel for a century. Now that we are building ours, you change the rules."
* **EU's Rebuttal:** "The planet doesn't care about history; it only cares about current emissions."

### üí° The Way Forward: "Green Hydrogen"

The only long-term fix is changing *how* steel is made.

* **Mission:** **National Green Hydrogen Mission**.
* **The Dream:** Replace "Coking Coal" with "Green Hydrogen" as the reducing agent.
* **Current Status:** Pilot projects by Tata Steel and Jindal are underway, but commercial scale is years away.

> **Final Thought:**
> CBAM is the first real test of "Climate Diplomacy." For India, it is a wake-up call: Decarbonize or get De-globalized. The steel factories of Odisha are now fighting a battle not just against competitors, but against a carbon ledger in Brussels.

# üèõÔ∏è The Global Architecture: Institutions, Agencies & Fora

### 1. The "Old Guard": United Nations Security Council (UNSC) üá∫üá≥

*The Horseshoe Table of Power*

* **Mandate:** Maintain international peace and security. It is the only UN body whose resolutions are **legally binding**.
* **Structure:**
* **P5 (Permanent 5):** US, UK, France, Russia, China (Have **Veto Power**).
* **E10 (Elected 10):** Non-permanent members elected for 2 years (No Veto).


* **The India Angle (2025 Status):**
* **The Demand:** India (along with **G4 nations** ‚Äì Brazil, Germany, Japan) demands a Permanent Seat.
* **The Blockade:** The **"Coffee Club"** (Pakistan, Italy, South Korea, etc.) opposes G4.
* **Latest Update (Sept 2025):** The G4 Ministers met in New York and pushed for "Text-Based Negotiations." The US, France, and Russia support India "in principle," but China remains the silent wall.



---

### 2. The "Money Bags": IMF vs. World Bank üí∞

*The Bretton Woods Twins (Washington D.C.)*

These two are often confused. Remember: **IMF is the Firefighter (Crises); World Bank is the Architect (Development).**

#### **A. International Monetary Fund (IMF)**

* **Mandate:** Global financial stability & Balance of Payments (BoP) crises.
* **Structure:**
* **Quotas:** A country's power = Its money contribution.
* **Special Drawing Rights (SDR):** The IMF's currency (Basket of $, ‚Ç¨, ¬£, ¬•, Yuan).


* **The India Angle:**
* **Voting Share:** India holds **2.76%** votes (China has 6.4%).
* **The Grievance:** In the **16th Quota Review (2024)**, the IMF increased the *capital* by 50% but **did not change the voting share**. India argues this is "undemocratic" as it doesn't reflect India's current economic size.



#### **B. World Bank Group (WBG)**

* **Mandate:** Poverty reduction & long-term loans for infrastructure (Dams, Schools).
* **5 Arms:**
* **IBRD:** Loans to middle-income countries (India is the largest borrower).
* **IDA:** Interest-free loans to the poorest nations.
* **IFC:** Loans to *Private Companies*.
* **MIGA:** Insurance against war/political risk.
* **ICSID:** Dispute settlement (India is **NOT** a member).


* **The India Angle:** India is the biggest client of the World Bank but pushes for "Climate Finance" at lower rates.

### 4. The "Battlefield": World Trade Organization (WTO) ‚öñÔ∏è

* **Mandate:** Regulate global trade, cut tariffs, and settle disputes.

* **The Result:** A deadlock. India successfully blocked any anti-farmer rules but didn't get a permanent solution either.
